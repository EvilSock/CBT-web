Jaco Rossouw, clinical psychologist, has been in private practice in Cape Town, South Africa for the last 18 years. He consults with children, adolescents, adults and families, from a cognitive-behavioural therapy approach. Jaco is a founding member of the Centre for Cognitive Behaviour Therapy. He is internationally certified as a cognitive behaviour therapist and is an Associate Fellow of the Albert Ellis Institute. He is involved in developing and providing training in CBT to other mental health professionals. A longstanding interest in the CBT treatment of PTSD has culminated in a research project on treatment of adolescents with PTSD. Jaco is also involved in other research projects as a member of the Centre for CBT

Areas of Interest:

  • Anxiety specific to Children (childhood fears, excessive worrying, exam and test anxiety)

Bradley Drake is registered as a clinical psychologist with the Health Professions Council of South Africa. He is a founding member of the Centre for Cognitive Behaviour Therapy, Cape Town, South Africa. Bradley is now in his 12th year in private practice and consults predominantly with adults and adolescents but also sees children. He is internationally certified as a cognitive behaviour therapist and is an Associate Fellow of the Albert Ellis Institute in New York City. He has additional specialist training from the Beck Institute in Philadelphia and is a member of the Association for Behavioural and Cognitive Therapies (New York). Bradley has been responsible for the creation and delivery of board certified CBT training workshops for professional therapists across South Africa since 2008. In addition to contributing toward a number of recent publications, he is involved in a number of research projects that the Centre for CBT is currently associated with. The launch of the Bimonthly Meeting for the Advancement of Behavioural and Cognitive Therapies for professionals in Cape Town represents his most recent initiative within the professional community.
